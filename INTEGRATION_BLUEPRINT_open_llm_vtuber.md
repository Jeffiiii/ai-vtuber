---

## The core principle — generated, not authored

You cannot produce an inner life by **authoring** one. Writing thinking and emotion into prompts and
training data makes her *perform* having them; it does not give her them. This is the central lesson
of the current `ai-vtuber` code, and it's worth stating plainly because it governs every decision
below.

`V2_1_THINKING.md` is titled "making Elysia *think*, not perform" — and its method is to train her on
thinking-shaped examples and prompt her to "actually think, ask real questions, change your mind."
That **is** performing. "Be authentic" as a training target yields authentic-*shaped* text, not
authenticity. Training on examples of thinking installs the *surface features* of thinking — the
question-back, the half-finished sentence, the late-night existential murmur — with nothing
underneath that produced them. It paints motion lines on a still object: the depiction of movement
without movement. The same is true of VIVID's mood tags and the Director's "your mood is playful, let
it color your tone": that is acting a feeling on cue, not having one.

The tell that something is *authored* rather than *real* is that **nothing caused it**. When she
"wonders whether memory makes someone real," she says it because a directive fired and the training
made that text likely — not because any process in her was wondering about *this* moment. Authored
inner lives are also finite: a deck of five `_THREADS` plus a menu of introspection directives, and
the audience eventually sees the deck.

**Neuro's inner life is *generated*, not authored** — the visible trace of a capable model situated
in a real, stateful, real-time world it is actually perceiving and acting in. She's genuinely playing
the game, so "I keep missing this jump" is a reaction to a real miss; she reads live chat as it
scrolls; she carries real accumulated state forward. Every thoughtful-sounding line is *caused by*
and *about* a real, current situation. Her trained *voice* (a chaotic, opinionated disposition) is a
**lens she reacts through**, applied to a real changing world — not a generator of self-contained
monologues.

So the asymmetry that drives this whole plan:

> **Authored** inner life is finite, decoupled from cause, and eventually transparent.
> **Generated** inner life is open-ended, grounded, and robust — because it can only ever say what
> some real, present state of the world caused it to say.

**The rule, applied everywhere below:** do not author her cognition or emotion. Build the *substrate*
whose visible trace is thinking and emotion, and let them emerge. **Fine-tuning's entire job is
voice** — how Elysia *phrases* a reaction — never *what* she thinks or feels in the moment.

Two honest limits, so this isn't oversold: flipping the paradigm is *necessary* (performance never
reaches Neuro), but the *degree* of realness still rides on (1) brain quality — an 8GB / 4B reacting
to a live situation has a ceiling — and (2) how rich the situation you give her is. The paradigm
unlocks the right *kind* of realness; the *amount* is bounded by the world you build and the model
that reacts to it.

## The engine: situated reactivity (two arcs)

If the inner life is generated by reacting to a real situation, then **the situation is the engine.**
Memory, mood, and autonomy are *state* — they make her reactions continuous and personal — but state
without a real situation to react to just yields a more sophisticated monologue. So the architecture
is built around a real, perceivable, stateful world she is present in:

```
  REAL ENVIRONMENT            CONSEQUENTIAL STATE            GENERATED REACTION
  (what she reacts to)        (how reactions accumulate)     (her, reacting)
  ───────────────────         ──────────────────────         ──────────────────
  screen  (vision OCR/VLM)                                    a capable model,
  live chat / events     ──►  memory (drives recall)    ──►  through her voice,
  (later) a task she's        mood    (caused by events)      reacting to the real
  genuinely doing             preoccupations (emergent)       present situation
```

You're building this in **two arcs** (your call: *both, phased*):

- **Arc 1 — Reactive presence (now, current hardware).** She genuinely *perceives and reacts to* a
  live situation — the screen, chat, events — in real time, with memory and consequential mood. This
  is already a categorical jump from performance, and it's achievable on the 4060.
- **Arc 2 — Situated agency (later, gated on a stronger brain).** She actually *does* a task with
  stakes — game-playing, à la Neuro's real engine. This is where your `games/` work becomes central.
  It's a major second-system lift; it waits until the reactive core is solid and the brain is better.

**Keep the engine character-agnostic.** The goal is both a private companion *and*, later, a public
VTuber — and because Elysia is a HoYoverse character, the public version must be an **original**
character. (Private use and *small, unmonetized* streaming tests can stay Elysia; do **not** monetize
her or grow an audience around the Elysia identity — switch to the original character before anyone is
asked to pay or to care.) The consequence for *this* build: keep the **character** layer (persona
text, voice, Live2D rig — the `elysia.json` assets) cleanly separable from the **engine** (memory,
mood, scheduler, perception). Then the eventual character swap is config-and-assets, not surgery.
Hold that seam from day one; your existing persona/engine split already points the right way.

## Workstream B — `ReactiveMemoryAgent` + memory (consequential state)

The state that makes her reactions continuous and personal. Memory must **genuinely drive** what she
says — real recall, relevant *now*, in the actual context the model reasons over — never injected to
*perform* "remembering." Pattern: **generative agents** (Park et al.) — a memory stream with
importance scores; retrieval ranked by relevance + recency + importance; periodic reflection.

> **Start from what you built.** `ai-vtuber/aivtuber/memory.py` already gives you the *operator-profile
> layer* — a durable per-viewer digest (summary + facts + `attachment`) injected with the right
> "don't recite; let it color you" discipline — and its docstring marks the FAISS seam. So B is **two
> layers, not a rewrite**: (1) **keep** the profile digest ("who this person is to me"), and (2) **add**
> the memory-stream + ranked retrieval below ("what I specifically recall right now"). Port
> `LongTermMemory`'s profile / `attachment` / `recall_text` / `set_profile` into the operator pool;
> build the stream fresh.

### B.1 The agent class

New `src/open_llm_vtuber/agent/agents/reactive_memory_agent.py`, implements the 3-method interface,
reuses the inherited decorator stack:

```python
class ReactiveMemoryAgent(AgentInterface):
    def __init__(self, llm, system, live2d_model, tts_preprocessor_config=None,
                 faster_first_response=True, segment_method="pysbd",
                 interrupt_method="user", memory_config=None, **kwargs):
        super().__init__()
        self._llm = llm; self._system = system            # persona = VOICE only, kept light
        self._live2d_model = live2d_model
        self._tts_preprocessor_config = tts_preprocessor_config
        self._faster_first_response = faster_first_response
        self._segment_method = segment_method
        self.interrupt_method = interrupt_method
        self._window = []                                  # short transcript buffer
        self._memory = MemoryStore(memory_config)          # profile layer + stream (B.2–B.8)
        self._mood = MoodModel(memory_config)              # C
        self._percept = None                               # latest perceived situation (E)
        self.chat = self._chat_factory()

    def handle_interrupt(self, heard_response: str) -> None:
        # reuse basic_memory_agent: rewrite last assistant turn to the heard fragment + "..."
        # + an "[Interrupted by user]" marker. Being cut off is a real event:
        self._mood.apply_event("interrupted")             # C — caused, not declared
        ...

    def set_memory_from_history(self, conf_uid, history_uid) -> None:
        self._window = self._load_window(get_history(conf_uid, history_uid))
        self._memory.load(conf_uid)

    def _chat_factory(self):
        @tts_filter(self._tts_preprocessor_config)
        @display_processor()
        @actions_extractor(self._live2d_model)
        @sentence_divider(faster_first_response=self._faster_first_response,
                          segment_method=self._segment_method, valid_tags=["think"])
        async def chat_impl(input_data: BatchInput):
            messages = self._build_messages(input_data)    # B.9 — assembles REAL state
            full = ""
            async for event in self._llm.chat_completion(messages, self._system):
                chunk = event.get("text","") if isinstance(event, dict) else event
                if chunk: full += chunk; yield chunk
            if full: await self._after_turn(input_data, full)   # B.4 consolidation + C update
        return chat_impl
```

Register via one `elif` in `agent_factory.py` (pass `memory_config` from agent settings).

### B.2 Memory item

`MemoryItem {id, kind ("episodic"|"semantic"|"preoccupation"), text, embedding, importance (1–10),
created_at, last_accessed, access_count, pool ("operator"|"ambient"), source, activation (preoccup.
only), superseded_by, metadata}`.

### B.3 Storage (local, offline)

sqlite for rows/text; **FAISS** (or sqlite-vec/Chroma) for vectors, one index per pool; a **local
bilingual** embedding model (bge-small bilingual, or `nomic-embed-text` via Ollama). `load(conf_uid)`
rehydrates on connect.

### B.4 Write = consolidation (not store-everything)

After a turn / on idle, a lightweight LLM pass over the recent window: **extract** durable statements
(most turns yield 0–2), **score importance**, **dedup/merge** by embedding similarity, **detect
contradictions** (set `superseded_by`), **insert** into the correct pool. Store-everything + blind
top-k is the naive trap that made OLV pull its own memory.

### B.5 Read = ranked retrieval

`score = w_rel·cos(query, m) + w_rec·decay^hours_since(last_accessed) + w_imp·importance/10`. Exclude
superseded; take top-k within a token budget; bump `last_accessed`/`access_count`; weight operator >
ambient.

### B.6 Two pools

**Operator** (your relationship — deep, primary, persistent, consolidated; includes the profile
digest + `attachment`) and **ambient** (stream/observed — shallow, lower-weight, aggressively
forgotten). Pool set at write from source / `from_name` / metadata.

### B.7 Preoccupations (emergent — replaces `_THREADS`)

A `MemoryItem(kind="preoccupation")` with `activation ∈ [0,1]`. **Created by the consolidation pass
when a theme actually recurs** (not hardcoded). Each scheduler tick `activation *= decay`; a related
event sets `activation = min(1, activation + boost)`. High-activation preoccupations are injected as
"what's genuinely on her mind" and are the *grounding* for proactive speech (D) — she returns to a
thought because it's actually active, not because a list says so.

### B.8 Forgetting

Periodically prune/demote low-importance + low-recency + low-access items (aggressive on ambient,
gentle on operator). Forgetting keeps recall sharp.

### B.9 Message assembly (`_build_messages`) — assembles *real* state

Layered, within a hard token budget (the 8GB pinch bites here):

```
[ system ]   persona (VOICE only) + interrupt-signal note
[ state ]    mood note derived from real mood state (C)  +  active preoccupations (B.7)
             +  retrieved long-term memories (B.5, budgeted)
[ world ]    the perceived situation, if any (E) — what she's seeing / what just happened
[ window ]   recent transcript turns
[ input ]    the new BatchInput
```

Everything in `[state]` and `[world]` is *real* — caused, current, consequential — not authored
performance. Keep the window short; recall comes from retrieval.

**Done when:** she recalls things across sessions *appropriately* (relevant, not stale, not dredged),
and the recall visibly shapes her replies.

## Workstream C — Mood (consequential affect, not a stage-direction)

Mood is **real state** iff it is (a) *caused* by actual events, (b) *persistent* across time, and
(c) *consequential* — it changes what she does. The anti-pattern isn't "telling the model its mood";
it's a mood that's a **random drift** or injected to *manufacture* a feeling that nothing caused.
A tone note *derived from genuinely-caused, persistent mood state* is legitimate conditioning;
a tone note from a dice-roll label is performance. Build the former.

```python
class MoodModel:
    # fast layer: valence ∈ [-1,1], arousal ∈ [0,1] (Russell circumplex)
    # slow layer:  attachment (per operator, from memory.py) — relationship warmth
    def tick(self):                      # decay toward baseline (called by the scheduler)
        self.valence += (self.baseline_v - self.valence)*self.decay
        self.arousal += (self.baseline_a - self.arousal)*self.decay
    def apply_event(self, kind, intensity=1.0):          # CAUSED, never declared on a timer
        dv, da = EVENT_AFFECT_MAP[kind]   # praised:(+.3,+.1) · interrupted:(-.1,+.2) · long_absence_return:(+.2,+.15)
        self.valence = clamp(self.valence + dv*intensity, -1, 1)
        self.arousal = clamp(self.arousal + da*intensity, 0, 1)
    def note(self) -> str | None:         # reflects REAL current state; may be None
        ...
    def expression(self) -> str | None:   # → Actions.expressions → Live2D (emotion.py vocabulary)
        ...
```

- **Surface it through behavior first, words second.** The honest outputs of mood are *what she
  attends to* (it can bias which memories surface and whether she initiates) and *her face*
  (`expression()` → Live2D). The tone `note()` is the lightest surface and is optional — include it
  only because it reflects real state, and keep it spare.
- **Two layers.** Fast event-caused valence/arousal over slow `attachment` (from `memory.py`,
  per operator). Wire `apply_event` from real outcomes: praise, interruption (B.1), a long absence
  then return, recalling a high-importance memory, a salient perceived event (E).

## Workstream D — Reactive autonomy (generated & grounded — no scripts)

OLV's "proactive speak" is a single prompt + flag on a trigger. Keep the **emission path**; replace
the **trigger** with a scheduler driven by real state — and replace the **content** mechanism
entirely. **No directive menu** ("perform a wonder / a reflection"); the model is never told to act
out an inner-life move. It is given *what is genuinely active or unaddressed right now* and generates
its own reaction as itself.

```python

            if trigger:
                await ctx.initiate_proactive(trigger)   # via OLV's proactive_speak path

        # No scripted introspection types.
        ...
```

- **Keep `director.py`'s reactive skeleton:** the `decide()` priority ladder (events → waiting

## Workstream E — Perception & the real environment (the engine)

This is what makes her lines *reactions to real stimuli* rather than recitations. Arc-1 sources:

- **Screen vision — reuse your `vision/` (OCR + VLM).** Run it on the screen/region, turn frames into
  a short structured observation ("the player just died"; "a new song started"), and feed that as the
  perceived situation into the agent (`_percept`, surfaced in B.9 `[world]`). This is your existing
  `director.add_observation` idea, now feeding the *real* agent instead of a scripted directive. Your
  text model can't see; the VLM/OCR is the bridge — caption to text, feed the text.
- **Live chat + events** arrive as `BatchInput` through OLV. Treat them as perception: salience (D)
  decides whether they warrant a reaction; mood reacts to charged ones (C).
- **Three authority modes for chat** (reasoning in `V3_DIRECTION`): **Ambient** (default — observed
  flavor in the ambient pool; can't drive/jailbreak; use `skip_memory`/`skip_history` + salience),
  **Addressable** (opt-in — needs a reply-selection + safety layer; off by default; prompt-injection
  surface), **Director-level** (out of scope until toggled).
- **Twitch — port your `twitch_source.py`** onto OLV's `LivePlatformInterface` (mirror
  `bilibili_live.py`; OLV ships Bilibili). It's a *stimulus source*, not an audience/influence system.
- **Web access — three layers, decided separately by the usual test (a real situation to react to? is
  it the bottleneck?):** *(a) Temporal grounding now* — current date/time/weather; trivial, removes
  the training-cutoff bubble; do it early. *(b) Curated ambient feed* — a few sources **you pick** she
  can ambiently react to; a real, changing world like the screen, and controllable because it's
  curated; a strong mid-term perception source. *(c) On-demand search* — she invokes it when a fact
  actually needs it (via OLV's MCP/tool support); bounded, and **brain-gated** (a 4B may add noise
  more than insight, so its value rises after a brain upgrade); later. *Open autonomous browsing /
  social feeds* — defer or avoid (marginal gain over a curated feed, wide injection surface, character
  pollution). **Discipline for all of it:** web content is *data to summarize and react to, never
  instructions to follow* (untrusted — the same prompt-injection surface as addressable chat), and she
  takes **no consequential actions** on it.

**Why this is the engine:** without a real situation to perceive, even perfect memory + mood is a
better monologue generator. Perception is what turns state into *response*.

**Safety Concerns** Private and single-user → no content limits needed; spontaneity is the priority and unrestricted is fine. Restriction layer designed later before stream stage.

## Workstream G — Situated agency: game-playing (the later arc)

Neuro's actual engine: she's *doing* a real task with stakes, so her reactions are about real events
in that task. This is the deepest realness lever and a **major second-system lift** — effectively a
separate game-playing capability plus a channel for it to inform her reactions. It is **deliberately
deferred** until the reactive core is solid and the brain is stronger (it asks more of the model than
chat does).

- **Seed:** your `games/` (tic-tac-toe / guessing / external) is the start — a real game loop she can
  act in and react to.
- **Integration shape (when you get here):** the game produces real events ("I just lost a piece";
  "the player blundered"), which flow into **E** as perceived situation; her reactions are generated
  against that real state, with **mood** moving on wins/losses and **memory** accumulating running
  bits. The game-playing decision-making itself may want a separate policy/model from her voice model.
- **Don't over-scope it now.** It's on the roadmap as Arc 2; the value of naming it is to keep Arc 1
  pointed at it (perception and state are built so a real task can plug into them later).

## 10. Open decisions & risks

- **Brain quality is now the central bound, not a footnote.** Situated reactivity needs a model that
  can genuinely reason about a live situation. The 4B has a ceiling; treat the rent-vs-API upgrade as
  the lever that raises Arc-1's quality and unlocks Arc-2. Generated realness ≠ unlimited realness.
- **Resist re-authoring.** The pull toward "just add a few good lines / a thinking prompt" will
  recur. Every time, the answer is to strengthen the *substrate* (perception, memory, caused mood),
  not to write the performance. If you find yourself editing what she *says*, you're in the old trap.
- **Embedding model / vector store:** pick a bilingual local embedder + FAISS/sqlite-vec before B.3
  (sets the vector dim).
- **Vision cost/cadence:** run VLM/OCR off the hot path at a sane interval; cache; don't block replies.
- **Consolidation latency:** run the memory write-pass after the turn / on idle, never inline.
- **Arc-2 scope:** game-playing may need its own model/policy distinct from the voice model — plan
  for that seam, don't force one model to both *be* her and *play* well.
